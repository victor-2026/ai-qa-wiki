# QA in the AI Era: Adapting, Evolving, and Thriving

## Key Message

> "AI doesn't replace human QA — it changes how QA works."

GenAI reshapes testing but presents challenges: social impacts, technical complexities, ethical dilemmas.

## Social Impacts of AI

### 1. AI Affects How We Speak
- Humans imitate LLM language patterns
- Risk: reduced linguistic diversity
- Risk: reinforced stereotypes from biased training data

### 2. AI Reduces Knowledge Sharing
- 25% drop in Stack Overflow activity after ChatGPT
- AI interactions don't add to shared knowledge pool
- Risk: weaker training data for future models

## Mitigating Social Risks

### Company Level
- **Training programs** for testers on AI methodologies
- **Inclusive test design** with diverse teams
- **Stakeholder engagement** including end users

### Team Level
- Balance GenAI with human expertise
- Use AI for repetitive tasks (log scanning, doc drafts)
- Workshops for collaboration
- AI "sika" sessions for open discussion

### Individual Level
- Don't skip real-world communication
- Validate AI outputs through collaboration
- Stay relevant: understand AI foundations

## Technical Challenges

### Data Consent Crisis
- GDPR, CCPA limit data access
- Users reluctant to share personal info
- QA must ensure data anonymization

### Synthetic Data Risks
- **Model collapse** — training on synthetic data amplifies errors
- Loss of diversity in datasets
- Must combine with real-world data + validation

## Technical Mitigation

1. **Collaborate with experts** — domain experts validate AI outputs
2. **Don't blindly trust AI** — clear acceptance criteria, automated + exploratory testing
3. **Invest in strong basics** — foundational skills before AI tools

## Ethical Considerations

### Lack of Standardized Benchmarks
- No universal framework for GenAI evaluation
- Must tailor metrics to specific domains

### Security Concerns
- Data leaks from training data
- Adversarial attacks
- Supply chain risks (third-party APIs)
- Follow OWASP Top 10 for LLM APIs

## Ethical Mitigation

- Establish clear benchmarks
- Address bias transparently (regular audits)
- Resource optimization (smaller models, off-peak testing)
- Continuous oversight with human judgment

## Key Insight

> "AI in QA represents progress over perfection."

Balance: human strengths (intuition, critical thinking) + AI capabilities (speed, pattern detection)
